Where the real decisions are

The thatch
Not if. When — and what it costs when it arrives.
›

Long straw re-done in 2014. A proper job runs 20–30 years. Your replacement window opens somewhere between 2034 and 2044 — comfortably within a standard ownership horizon. This is not speculation. It is a calendar entry.

Full re-thatch on a property this scale: £28,000–£45,000, specialist only, Listed Building Consent required. You cannot use a cheaper material. Budget for it before you complete. Not after.

The heating
Oil dependency with nowhere to run.
›

You are heating a 17th-century structure with lime walls, flagstone floors, and exposed timber frames using an oil-fired AGA. The Band G EPC is not negligence — it is physics. Cavity wall insulation: off the table. External insulation: off the table. Internal insulation requires LBC and destroys the character of the rooms.

The building breathes the way it was built to breathe. The government's oil boiler phase-out arrives around 2035. The conversation about what replaces this system is not optional — only its timing is up to you.

The listing
What Grade II means when you try to do anything.
›

New window: Listed Building Consent. New kitchen: LBC. New flue: LBC. The process takes 8–12 weeks and costs £800–£2,500 in professional fees before a single tradesperson arrives. Every approved work then costs 20–30% more than its equivalent in an unlisted property.

This is not a deterrent. It is the price of acquiring something genuinely rare. If you came for the yield, this is the wrong building. If you came for the building itself — you already knew all of this, and you came anyway.
